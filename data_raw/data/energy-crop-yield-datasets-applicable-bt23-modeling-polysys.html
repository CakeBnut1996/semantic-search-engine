Energy Crop Yield Datasets applicable to BT23 modeling (POLYSYS)

Yield (i.e., tons of biomass per acre per year) is a key driver of production potential for many biomass resources. For agricultural resources, crop- and county-specific yields are an input to the economic modeling used to assess biomass production capacity in the BT23. Yields for agricultural biomass resources were derived from field trials from the Sun Grant Initiative Regional Feedstock Partnership, which served the basis for calibration of county yields (see 2016 Billion-Ton Report section 4.2.4)
For the 2016 and 2023 Billion-Ton reports, a workflow was established to provide a series of yields including biophysical, harvestable potential, future year- and scenario-specific potential, stand-age specific potential, and final solution yields. These datasets are comprised of several yield types including 1) PRISM Yield, 2) Base Harvestable Mean Annual Increment, 3) Mature Harvestable Yield (or MAI) by scenario, 4) Harvestable Yield for the Complete Crop Rotation by Scenario, and 5) Solution Yield by Scenario. Each yield type is defined below in the metadata.

Metadata
1) PRISM Yield

This dataset provides yield output from PRISM by resource and FIPS code. For herbaceous energy resources, it includes the average annual mature above-ground yield. For woody resources, such as poplar and willow, it provides the mean annual increment (MAI).

Note: This dataset does not include harvest efficiency, scenario improvement factors, planting year, harvest year, or the annual yield factor.

File: agyield_prism.csv.zip

Field definitions are as follows:

resource: crop or resource.

FIPS: county FIPS code.

yieldPRISM: theoretical mature annual yield from PRISM for the county FIPS code.

yieldUnit: yield value unit.

_id_: identifier.



2) Base Harvestable MAI

This dataset contains the average annual yield over the rotation period by resource and FIPS code, for both herbaceous and woody energy resources. It is derived from the PRISM yield data and incorporates the annual yield factor and harvest efficiency. However, it does not account for scenario-based improvements.

Yield = PRISM yield X Harvest Efficiency X Average Annual Yield Factor



File: agyield_base_harv_mai.csv.zip

Field definitions are as follows:

resource: crop or resource.

FIPS: county FIPS code.

yieldPRISM: theoretical mature annual yield from PRISM. This value can be found in the PRISM yield table, and it is used in this yield calculation.

harvestEfficiency: harvest efficiency for the crop.

meanAnnualYieldFactor: yield factor from the Mean Annual Yield Factor table for the crop and FIPS.

yield: calculated by multiplying the PRISM yield, harvest efficiency, and average annual yield factor.

yieldUnit: yield value unit.

_id_: identifier

5) Solution Yield by Scenario

This dataset provides the calculated yield from POLYSYS output files for energy resources. It has been divided into two files:

Files: agyield_solution_herb.csv.zip, agyield_solution_srwc.csv.zip,



The solution yield is derived from POLYSYS solution output files. The yield for each resource, simulation, and year is calculated by dividing the total amount harvested ("PRODUCTION") by the total acres harvested ("HARVEST") from the POLYSYS production files:

Yield [scenario, year] = Tons harvested [year] / Acres harvested [year]

This dataset includes the scenarios for each price for the Low, Medium, and High scenarios.

Field definitions are as follows:

resource: crop or resource.

siml: scenario ran including price offered.

year: simulation year.

FIPS: county FIPS code.

footprintArea: total footprint area of the resource that simulation year.

harvestedArea: total area harvested of the resource that simulation year.

production: amount of feedstock harvested that simulation year.

yield: comes from POLYSYS solution output and is calculated annually by taking the production quantity divided by the harvested area.

landSource: land source.

footprintAreaUnit: footprint area unit.

harvestedAreaUnit: harvested area units.

productionUnit: production quantity units.

yieldUnit: yield value unit.

_id_: identifier.



Definitions

Harvest Efficiency refers to the portion of total yield that is successfully harvested, accounting for losses due to harvesting methods. It is used to adjust the yield calculation by reflecting the actual amount of feedstock that could be harvested from the potential yield.

Annual Yield Factor refers to a multiplier used in yield calculations to adjust the total yield based on the age of the crop at the time of harvest. It reflects the expected changes in crop productivity as the crop matures, modifying the yield for each specific harvest age throughout the crop’s total rotation.

Average Annual Yield Factor is a factor used to account for the average yield over the entire rotation period of the crop, considering variations in yield across different harvest ages. It averages the yield factors for each year of the crop's rotation to estimate the overall yield potential over the rotation.
